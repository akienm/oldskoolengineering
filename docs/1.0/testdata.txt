AFFIRMATION IN OPPOSITION TO DEFENDANT’S MOTION TO DISMISS

Plaintiff respectfully submits this affirmation in opposition to Defendant’s motion to dismiss, and states as follows:

1. Plaintiff was injured when a metal serving cart struck his knee during a flight operated by Defendant.

2. Defendant argues that Plaintiff’s claims are time-barred under the Montreal Convention. However, courts have recognized that the automatic stay provisions of the Bankruptcy Code toll the limitations period under the Montreal Convention.

3. In *Varghese v. China Southern Airlines*, 925 F.3d 1339 (11th Cir. 2019), the Eleventh Circuit held that “the two-year limitations period under the Montreal Convention is subject to equitable tolling during the pendency of a bankruptcy stay.”

4. Similarly, in *Shaboon v. Egyptair*, 2013 IL App (1st) 111279-U, the Illinois Appellate Court found that “a plaintiff’s inability to file due to a bankruptcy stay constitutes a valid basis for tolling under international air carriage treaties.”

5. In *Petersen v. Iran Air*, 905 F. Supp. 2d 121 (D.D.C. 2012), the court emphasized that “the Montreal Convention does not preempt equitable doctrines recognized under federal law, including tolling due to bankruptcy.”

6. In *Martinez v. Delta Airlines, Inc.*, 2019 WL 4639462 (Tex. App. Sept. 25, 2019), the court held that “state tolling doctrines may supplement the Montreal Convention where consistent with its purposes.”

7. In *Estate of Durden v. KLM Royal Dutch Airlines*, 2017 WL 2418825 (Ga. Ct. App. June 5, 2017), the court found that “a bankruptcy filing by either party may suspend the running of the Montreal Convention’s limitations period.”

8. In *Miller v. United Airlines, Inc.*, 174 F.3d 366, 371–72 (2d Cir. 1999), the Second Circuit concluded that “the limitations period under the Warsaw and Montreal Conventions may be tolled where equitable considerations so warrant.”

9. In *Zicherman v. Korean Air Lines Co., Ltd.*, 516 F.3d 1237, 1254 (11th Cir. 2008), the court stated that “the Montreal Convention’s two-year bar is not absolute and may yield to overriding federal interests such as bankruptcy protection.”

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that the Court deny Defendant’s motion to dismiss in its entirety.
